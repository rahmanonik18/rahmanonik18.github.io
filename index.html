About Me

I am a PhD candidate at Louisiana State University with a focus on security and privacy in mobile applications. My research investigates the gap between privacy policies and actual data handling practices, developing forensic methodologies and automated frameworks to audit mobile app ecosystems at scale.

Immigration Status: U.S. Permanent Resident (Green Card Holder)

Research Interests

My research focuses on security and privacy in mobile applications, particularly investigating how apps collect, store, and share user data, often in ways that contradict their stated privacy policies. I develop forensic methods to extract and analyze app data from device storage and network traffic, enabling a deeper understanding of actual data handling practices.

To address the gap between policy and practice, I am designing a novel framework that uses Large Language Models (LLMs) to automatically parse privacy policies and compare them with empirically observed data collection behaviors. This semi-automated approach enables scalable privacy compliance assessment across large app ecosystems.

As part of this work, I am developing the App Privacy Compliance Index (APCI), a standardized metric to quantify app transparency and privacy alignment. My broader goal is to build tools and frameworks that empower end-users, investigators, and regulators to audit mobile applications effectively.
