I am a PhD candidate at Louisiana State University specializing in security and privacy in mobile applications. My research investigates the gap between privacy policies and actual data handling practices in mobile apps, working at the Baggil(i) Truth Lab (BiTlab).

Research Interests

My research focuses on security and privacy in mobile applications, particularly investigating how apps collect, store, and share user data, often in ways that contradict their stated privacy policies. I develop forensic methods to extract and analyze app data from device storage and network traffic.

I am designing a novel framework that uses Large Language Models (LLMs) to automatically parse privacy policies and compare them with empirically observed data collection behaviors. As part of this work, I am developing the App Privacy Compliance Index (APCI), a standardized metric to quantify app transparency.
